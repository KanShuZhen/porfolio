iPrice.my – Malaysia’s Largest Price Comparison Site

iPrice Group is a leading price comparison platform in Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Hong Kong and Australia), helping millions of users easily find the best deals across multiple e-commerce websites such as Lazada, Shopee, and Zalora. iPrice simplifies online shopping by aggregating products, vouchers, and promotions across different marketplaces, making product discovery and price transparency faster and earns revenue via an affiliate model.

My Role

  • Lead UX/UI Designer: across PLP, filter, homepage, and coupon redesign initiatives and apply design thinking framework to structure the redesign work.
  • User Research: Conducted interviews, usability tests, heuristic evaluation.
  • Prototyping: Created mobile-first prototypes in Figma.
  • A/B Testing: Designed experiments and analyzed results with Product Managers.
  • Cross-functional Collaboration: Worked closely with product managers, developers, stakeholder and marketing team.

Goals

Redesign key pages (Product Listing, Homepage, Coupons page) to address user pain points, streamline navigation, and boost conversion rates through data-driven A/B testing.
The project aimed to redesign the experience to:

  • Streamline navigation and discovery
  • Simplify filter usage
  • Personalize the homepage
  • Optimize coupon claiming flow
  • Boost conversion and engagement metrics

Understanding the Users

Discovery through User Interviews

We conducted user interviews with a diverse group of users, including price-conscious shoppers, frequent online buyers, and first-time visitors. Our goal was to empathize with real user pain points and validate assumptions before jumping into redesigning.

We carried out 10 interviews and 7 contextual inquiries (watching users interact with the existing platform). The interviews helped surface critical insights about users struggles with product discovery, filter complexity, and coupon redemption.

What Users Said

During user interviews and testing, several recurring frustrations emerged:
I dont want to think too much. Just show me the best option clearly.
Price differences between sellers are confusing. I just want a quick comparison I can trust.
“Its frustrating when I filter for a brand, but irrelevant products still show up.”
“Sometimes I see a coupon, but it doesnt even work at checkout.”

Problem

User Pain Points

  • Users found the Product Listing Page (PLP) overwhelming and the filters confusing
  • Homepage content was often seen as irrelevant or cluttered, leading to low engagement
  • The coupon flow was inefficient as users frequently abandoned carts after failing to redeem discounts easily

Stakeholder and Business Needs

  • Product owners wanted faster product discovery to drive higher conversions
  • Marketing teams needed the homepage and coupon pages optimized for promotional campaigns without sacrificing user experience
  • Need consideration of AMP requirements for performance and SEO ranking

Problem Statement

How might we simplify product discovery, make coupon usage effortless, and create a scalable homepage while balancing user experience and business goals?

How We Get There

With clear problems and user pain points defined, we moved into the ideation phase to explore possible solutions. We organized several cross-functional ideation workshops involving product managers, developer, product owners and marketers.
Using techniques like “Crazy 8s” sketching and “How Might We” sessions, we generated ideas, prioritized features, and quickly aligned on what could deliver the most business and user value.

Our ideation process

  • Crazy 8s Exercise: Each team member quickly sketched 8 ideas in 8 minutes to encourage divergent thinking. This helped surface a wide variety of solutions, from bold structural changes to smaller UI tweaks.
  • “How Might We” Sessions: Using statements like How might we make finding the right product effortless? and How might we make coupon use feel rewarding, not confusing? to reframe problems into opportunities.

Key solution ideas

  • Progressive filters
    Show only high-impact defaults (price, brand, discount) first, with advanced filters expandable on demand. Reduced cognitive load while keeping power-user control.
  • Sticky filters & sort controls for mobile
    Floating buttons kept controls accessible while scrolling, reducing friction for mobile shoppers.
  • Redesigned product cards
    Highlighted price drops, discounts, and trusted sellers upfront for quick, easy comparisons without excessive scrolling or reading.
  • Coupon carousel with urgency
    Featured popular coupons with expiry timers to drive redemption through FOMO.
  • Curated homepage modules
    Clean, modular layout focused on discovery and trust, including:
    Today’s Handpicked Deals: highlighting limited-time offers to create urgency
    Exclusive Coupons: curated for higher redemption
    Top Trending: leveraging social proof
    Selected Secondhand: presenting trusted secondhand products for value-driven shoppers.
    Game Top-ups from Trusted Stores: to grow audience segment with reliable digital products
    New Releases: for tech-savvy users
    Popular Categories: quick navigation by interest

This modular approach helped users quickly find relevant content, boosting engagement while keeping the layout clean and structured. It also gave the marketing team flexibility to update promotions without disrupting the experience. Each solution was mapped to a user pain point and prioritized for value and feasibility.

UI Design

Challenges

One of the key challenges I faced during my time at iPrice was navigating technical limitations due to the AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) framework, which restricted the use of interactive components and dynamic design patterns. This required me to think creatively and collaborate closely with developers and product manager to deliver solutions that maintained a smooth user experience while adhering to AMPs strict guidelines.

At the same time, the team operated on an agile methodology with tight, fast-moving sprints, which meant balancing design quality with rapid delivery. I prioritized MVP features first, then iteratively layered improvements based on feedback.

Results and Impacts

Our redesign delivered measurable improvements:

  • 18% increase in product listing page engagement
  • 12% increase in filter engagement as more users successfully refined their search)
  • 18% increase in coupon claims (Redesign coupon page: A/B test vs old UI)
  • 15% boost in homepage engagement (CTR) with personalized modules
  • Enhanced usability and customer satisfaction, leading to increased return visits
  • Faster load times with AMP-compliant pages supporting SEO improvements
  • User feedback: 78% found new PLP “easier to use” (post-launch survey)

Reflection

Working at iPrice strengthened my skills in constraint-based design, UX design thinking, and data-driven decision making. It taught me the importance of continuous validation through users, especially when operating under technical and timeline constraints.

Most importantly, it reinforced that thoughtful UX improvements, even small ones, can drive meaningful business impact at scale.
